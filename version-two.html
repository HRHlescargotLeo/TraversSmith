Worker status reform: what the Employment Rights Bill means in practice

Sarah Mitchell · Partner, Employment
14 May 2026 · 10 min read

The Employment Rights Bill received Royal Assent in April 2026, introducing the most significant reforms to employment law in a generation. This briefing sets out the key changes and what they mean for employers.

Background

The Bill consolidates and extends worker protections across several dimensions: the elimination of the three-tier worker status framework, enhanced unfair dismissal protections from day one of employment, and significant changes to zero-hours contract regulation.

Key changes

Two-tier status: the distinction between “worker” and “employee” is abolished for most purposes.
Day-one unfair dismissal: the two-year qualifying period is removed.
Zero-hours contracts: a right to guaranteed hours after 12 weeks of regular work.

Restructuring your workforce in 2026: a step-by-step playbook for in-house teams

Written for portal members only. Not published on traverssmith.com.
Sarah Mitchell · Partner, Employment
11 Jun 2026 · 14 min read

A practical, sequenced playbook for running a workforce restructuring under the new Employment Rights Bill regime — including the consultation triggers most in-house teams miss.

The five stages

1
Scope and headcount modelling — establishing the “establishment” for collective consultation thresholds, now assessed at group level.
2
Consultation strategy — electing representatives, the 30/45-day clocks, and documenting genuine consideration.
3
Selection criteria — objectivity, scoring, and the day-one unfair dismissal exposure.
4
Settlement and exit — where a protected conversation is safe, and where it is not.
5
Communications and press — coordinating with the crisis line if the restructuring is likely to attract attention.
